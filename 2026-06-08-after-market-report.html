💡 Today's Key Points
🤖

Tech Leads Recovery — MRVL Surges on S&P 500 Inclusion

Technology was the standout sector (+1.67%), bouncing strongly from Friday's chip-sector bloodbath. The dominant catalyst: Marvell Technology ($MRVL +9.63%) officially joined the S&P 500, triggering index-fund demand and a fresh wave of AI-infrastructure buying. The SOX index posted unanimous gains after its worst day in six years last Thursday.

🧬

TNGX +53%: Breakthrough Pancreatic Cancer Data Drives Day's Biggest Move

Tango Therapeutics ($TNGX) surged +52.97% — the session's top volume-surge mover — after announcing its vopimetostat/daraxonrasib combination achieved a 92% objective response rate in pancreatic cancer. Wolfe Research called the data "groundbreaking." The company quickly followed with a proposed $500M public offering at day's close.

🌐

Galaxy Digital +21% — AI Data Center Pivot Steals Crypto Rally

$GLXY surged +21.36% as CEO Mike Novogratz touted the "largest single-campus data center in America." Barron's noted the move is primarily an AI infrastructure play rather than pure crypto. Bitcoin also rebounded, dragging along crypto-adjacent names including Coinbase and Robinhood.

⚡

Broad Market Mixed — Nasdaq Bounces, Dow Slips

QQQ (+1.56%) and IWM (+0.87%) outperformed while DIA (-0.15%) slipped. The divergence reflects tech-led buying into Monday's rebound after Friday's sharp sell-off. SPY managed only a +0.23% recovery, suggesting the broader market remains cautious. Iran-Israel tensions and Trump commentary on government stakes in AI companies were the macro backdrop.

💛

Bonds Sold, Gold Held — Risk-On Undertone

TLT fell -0.52% (yields rose) as investors rotated out of Treasuries amid the equity rebound. Gold ($GLD +0.26%) held gains near $397, supported by geopolitical risk from the Iran-Israel situation. The concurrent sell in bonds and hold in gold signals a partial risk-on session rather than a full flight-to-safety environment.

⚠️

Utilities & REIT Weakness Flags Rate Sensitivity Concern

Rate-sensitive sectors led the decline: Utilities -1.70%, Real Estate -1.31%. Rising yields on the long end are squeezing these dividend-proxy sectors. Communication Services (-0.90%) also lagged, suggesting some rotation away from non-tech growth. Healthcare (-0.53%) dipped despite $TNGX and $OSCR strength — sector-wide selling offset individual biotech catalysts.

🗓️

Eyes on Oracle Wednesday — Key Tech Earnings Ahead

Oracle ($ORCL) reports Wednesday after the close (Jun 10 AMC). Price target consensus implies ~20% upside. Chewy ($CHWY) also reports Wednesday morning with a very bullish target price (+94%). These are the week's high-profile tests for enterprise tech and consumer spending durability.
