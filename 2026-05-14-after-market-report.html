Today's Key Points
  • 1. Broad Market Rally: All major indices closed higher on Thursday May 14, with the S&P 500 (+0.79%) and Nasdaq (+0.71%) pressing toward fresh record territory. The Dow crossed back above 42,000. Small-caps (IWM +0.64%) also participated, suggesting broad risk appetite rather than a narrow leadership move.
  • 2. CSCO Dominated Headlines: Cisco's 13.4% surge — its best single-day performance in roughly 25 years — was the dominant story of the session. Q3 FY2026 earnings delivered record quarterly revenue, a raised full-year outlook, and a $1B AI-focused restructuring plan including ~4,000 job cuts. The CEO described AI demand driving a "networking supercycle," which lifted adjacent names including Nokia and other networking infrastructure plays.
  • 3. Technology Sector Led All Groups (+1.88%): Tech's outperformance was broad-based with 148 individual stocks in confirmed uptrends. Semiconductors, networking, and AI-adjacent names all saw elevated activity.
  • 4. AI Optical / Photonics Theme Ignited POET (+43.15%): POET Technologies announced a wafer-level photonic integration partnership with Lumilens targeting next-generation AI optical networks, alongside reports of a potential $500M AI infrastructure deal. The stock saw 121M shares trade — nearly 2.5x recent daily average — the highest volume name in the surge screener.
  • 5. Transportation Sector Breakout: Trucking and logistics names surged with exceptional volume. KNX (Knight-Swift) +13.82% on 14.8M shares, SNDR (Schneider National) +13.85% on 3.2M shares. The moves suggest improving freight market sentiment or a positive sector catalyst.
  • 6. Clean Energy / Solar Bounce: SEDG (SolarEdge) +17.47%, ENPH (Enphase) +14.31% led a strong solar sector recovery, likely driven by improved policy sentiment and short-covering. Both stocks have been under pressure in recent months.
  • 7. ONDS Explosive Volume (+26.52%): Ondas Holdings reported Q1 2026 results with revenue surging +1,065% year-over-year, backed by its Palantir-affiliated autonomous drone business. Raised its full-year 2026 revenue forecast to $390M. Volume reached 240M shares — among the highest in the market today.
  • 8. AMAT After-Hours: +4.12%: Applied Materials released Q2 FY2026 results after the close, beating EPS estimates by 7.84% and revenue by 2.06%. The semiconductor equipment maker benefits from accelerating AI chip manufacturing capex. After-hours price moved from $440.56 to approximately $458.73.
  • 9. Bonds Flat, Gold Retreated: TLT +0.14% indicates muted bond market reaction despite the equity rally — yields were little changed. GLD fell 0.76% as risk-on sentiment reduced safe-haven demand; gold pulled back from recent highs.
  • 10. Basic Materials the Lone Significant Laggard (−1.43%): Commodity-sensitive materials stocks underperformed as the stronger-dollar environment and weaker commodity prices weighed on the sector.
