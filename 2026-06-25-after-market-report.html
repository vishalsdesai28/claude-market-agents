💡 Today's Key Points

  • 🧩 Micron Supercycle Signal: Micron reported a historic Q3 FY2026 with revenue quadrupling year-over-year and net profit rising approximately 15-fold, fueled by insatiable AI infrastructure demand. The company disclosed long-term supply agreements providing unprecedented forward revenue visibility ($50B). Analysts called the results "historic" and Susquehanna raised its price target to $2,000. MU surged +15.74% and overtook Meta and Tesla in market capitalization during the session.
  • 💻 Semiconductor Equipment Rally: Micron's blowout results created a powerful read-through for the entire semiconductor supply chain. Applied Materials (AMAT, +13.44%) introduced new systems for AI chip DRAM and advanced packaging. Lam Research (LRCX, +7.21%), Teradyne (TER, +10.48%), and MKS Instruments (MKSI, +7.52%) all surged on improved capex visibility. BofA noted the chip rally has "durable" characteristics with further room to run.
  • 📡 QCOM AI Data Center Catalyst: Qualcomm (QCOM, +3.71%) revealed the identity of its previously undisclosed "mystery big tech customer" in AI data center chips, igniting analyst upgrades. This confirmed QCOM's multi-year AI server opportunity and contributed to the session's broader semiconductor momentum.
  • 🏢 Industrials and Healthcare Led Broad Market: Industrials (+1.63%) led all sectors. Caterpillar (CAT, +6.29%) benefited from AI data center construction demand alongside infrastructure tailwinds. Healthcare ranked second (+1.43%), led by Kymera Therapeutics (KYMR, +16.61%) after completing Phase 2b BROADEN2 enrollment for KT-621 in atopic dermatitis, with topline data expected by year-end 2026.
  • 🏈 Gold Outperforms Bonds: GLD gained +0.97% as gold held near highs amid dollar uncertainty and today's May PCE data (ticked up to highest level since 2023). TLT slipped -0.03%, reflecting slight upward pressure on long yields as the PCE print reinforced the Fed's wait-and-see posture.
  • 📉 Mega-Cap Tech Acted as Drag: The Technology sector finished -0.18% despite semiconductor strength. Microsoft, Apple (whose MacBook/iPad pricing was raised due to skyrocketing memory costs per Reuters), and Nvidia (facing rising competition concerns per Barron's) all declined. Communication Services fell -1.04%. Major indexes SPY and DIA were essentially flat (+0.14%) due to this tension.
  • ⚡ Corning AI Fiber Opportunity: GLW surged +10.78% to fresh 52-week highs. Corning also declared a quarterly dividend. Nvidia's network ambitions in AI infrastructure are viewed as a multi-year demand driver for optical fiber. The stock benefited from both the Micron AI rally halo effect and its own distinct fiber-optic growth story.
  • 📋 Friday Outlook: No major large-cap earnings are scheduled for Friday June 26. Markets will likely consolidate around Micron-driven semiconductor momentum with potential follow-through in AMAT, LRCX, TER, and GLW. The next key earnings catalysts arrive June 30 with Nike (NKE) and Constellation Brands (STZ) reporting after the bell. Watch SK Hynix's Nasdaq listing announcement as an additional memory sector catalyst.
