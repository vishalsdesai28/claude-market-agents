📋 Executive Summary
U.S. equities finished the shortened Juneteenth-adjacent session with a tech-led rally, as AI infrastructure and clean-energy power names surged on strong institutional demand. The NASDAQ-100 (QQQ +2.51%) led all major indices, propelled by Bloom Energy (+15.41%), Astera Labs (+11.31%), and Marvell Technology (+7.27%). The Russell 2000 (IWM +1.97%) outperformed the large-cap S&P 500 (SPY +0.78%), signaling broad participation and a risk-on tone. The session also coincided with June quarterly options expiration (Quadruple Witching), contributing to elevated volume across the board.
Key macro theme: Markets largely shrugged off news that US-Iran nuclear talks stalled before beginning (Bloomberg), limiting the safe-haven bid in gold and leaving energy stocks under pressure. AI power infrastructure — nuclear, fuel cells, and advanced power electronics — continued to attract aggressive buying, led by Bloom Energy's +15% surge on strong institutional accumulation. Smith & Wesson (SWBI) gained +9.91% following Tuesday's after-close earnings beat (+60% EPS surprise). Goldman Sachs cut its year-end gold price target by $500 to $4,900, which contributed to GLD's weakness.
