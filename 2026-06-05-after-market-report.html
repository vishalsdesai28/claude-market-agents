💡 Today's Key Points

  • 📉Broad risk-off selloff dominated Friday's session. A semiconductor-led rout — headlined by a Reuters report that the chip selloff erased over $1 trillion in market value — dragged QQQ down 4.80% and the S&P 500 lower by 2.58%. The Dow Jones held up best (-1.35%) given its defensive mix, but no major index was spared.
  • ⚡Technology was the worst-performing sector at -6.11%, followed by Basic Materials (-5.05%). The selloff reflects concern about AI capex sustainability, potential export curbs, and stretched valuations in the semiconductor complex.
  • 🛡Defensive sectors were the only safe harbor. Consumer Defensive rose +1.60% — the lone sector with a meaningful gain — supported by rotation into KO, CCEP, FIZZ, and USFD. Real Estate (+0.57%), Utilities (+0.20%), and Healthcare (+0.15%) also outperformed, signaling risk-averse repositioning into the weekend.
  • 💰Gold fell alongside equities (-3.65% GLD), suggesting the selloff involved some margin-related deleveraging rather than pure flight-to-safety. TLT was also only marginally lower (-0.51%), with bonds not receiving the full flight-to-safety bid that might be expected in a larger equity breakdown.
  • 🔥Volume-surge standout: BBCP +30.70%. Concrete Pumping Holdings surged on Q2 earnings beat with strong revenue growth (confirmed via GlobeNewswire/GuruFocus earnings call highlights). ARCB +6.17% followed on a raised Q2 outlook for its LTL and asset-light segments (confirmed via FreightWaves). GIII +5.21% rallied on a Q1 FY2027 earnings beat plus raised guidance (confirmed via GlobeNewswire).
  • 📊Market breadth was notably narrow. Only 13 volume-surge stocks met the full quality filter (Small+ cap, 200-day MA above, RelVol >1.5x, price >+2%), with the overwhelming majority of those being defensive/non-tech names. Uptrend stock count at 501 reflects moderate deterioration from recent sessions.
  • 📅Next week's earnings focus shifts to Oracle (ORCL, Wed AMC) and Adobe (ADBE, Thu AMC). Both are high-profile tech names that will test whether the sector selloff was indiscriminate or fundamentally driven. CHWY has the highest analyst price target upside (+89.8%) among next-week reporters.
