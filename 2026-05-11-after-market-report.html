📋 Executive Summary

U.S. equities posted modest gains on Monday, May 11, with all three major indices advancing in a narrow, low-conviction session. The S&P 500 added 0.19% to 7,412.95 while the Dow Jones climbed 0.19% to 49,704.46 and the NASDAQ Composite edged up 0.08% to 26,267.79. Small-caps outpaced large-caps, with IWM rising 0.43%, signaling a tentative risk-on tilt.


The real story Monday was in individual names. Semiconductor and quantum-tech stocks dominated the tape — MRAM surged +47.68%, GSIT jumped +38.70%, IonQ (IONQ) advanced +15.54%, and Rocket Lab (RKLB) gained +11.26%. Qualcomm (QCOM) led mega-cap semis with +8.42% while Micron (MU) added +6.50%, continuing a multi-week tech squeeze driven by AI/HBM demand narratives and improving supply-chain signals. Intel (INTC) rose +3.62% on massive volume (178M shares). First Solar (FSLR) gained +6.06% on a clean-energy infrastructure bid. BMO reporters CRCL (+15.78%), Barrick Mining (+9.02%), and Fox Corp (FOXA +7.59% / FOX +8.09%) all saw strong session-day gains after pre-market beats. TLT fell -0.60% as longer yields crept higher despite index resilience, and VIX edged up to 18.36 — not alarming, but a reminder that the market remains vigilant about macro risks.
