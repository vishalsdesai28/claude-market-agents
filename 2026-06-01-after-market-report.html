⚡ Nvidia Computex 2026 Impact
Nvidia unveiled RTX Spark (ARM-based PC processor), directly challenging Intel/AMD in the Windows laptop market. Dell XPS 13 priced at $699 will feature the chip.
Vera Rubin NVL72 server rack delivered to CoreWeave — world's first deployment. Michael Dell confirmed Dell executed the delivery, driving CRWV +13.96% and DELL +10.70%.
ARM Holdings was the standout beneficiary (+15.73%) as Nvidia confirmed its next-generation PC chips will use Arm silicon architecture.
HPE unveiled Nvidia Vera CPU server purpose-built for Agentic AI, then posted Q2 earnings with EPS beat of +10.83% post-close, sending shares +20.87% in after-hours.
📊 Market Breadth & Rotation
Technology sector surged +2.96%, the only meaningful sector winner today, reflecting concentrated AI/semiconductor momentum rather than broad market participation.
Energy +1.75% was the only other positive sector. Iran peace hopes cited on Friday appear to have partially unwound, with energy finding support from separate demand drivers.
Utilities -2.63% and Consumer Cyclical -2.03% bore the brunt of rotation out of defensive/rate-sensitive names into technology. IWM (Russell 2000) -0.50% confirms small-cap underperformance.
Gold -1.40% (GLD) retreated sharply, suggesting a risk-on rotation away from safe-haven assets. 10Y+ Treasury bonds (TLT) also slipped -0.34%, a modest reflationary signal.
🔍 Software/Cloud Rally Details
Software names surged on the thesis that Nvidia's expanded AI compute platform (RTX Spark + Vera Rubin) accelerates demand for AI-native SaaS and cloud infrastructure.
Cybersecurity was particularly strong with OKTA +13.40% and PANW +6.67%; MarketWatch reported cybersecurity sector was approaching first record in nearly a year.
TWLO +19.36% driven by Investor's Business Daily "Stock of the Day" feature on AI Voice opportunity. Catalyst is non-earnings (momentum/analyst coverage).
SNOW +9.63%, DDOG +12.19%, NET +11.99% all rallied without specific catalysts beyond the broad AI/cloud tailwind from Nvidia's Computex announcements.

CRDO's after-hours decline of -13.41% despite beating EPS (+17.38%) suggests elevated valuation expectations in high-growth semi names. A "beat-and-sell" pattern warrants monitoring.
Upcoming earnings catalyst: CRWD reports Wed 6/3 AMC — a high-profile cybersecurity name. Sector already near record; strong results could extend the run or trigger profit-taking.
