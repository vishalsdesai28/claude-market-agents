🇺🇸 U.S. Stock Market Analysis

📅 2026-07-01 (Wednesday) – Final After-Market Data

📋 Executive Summary

U.S. equities pulled back modestly on Wednesday, July 1 — the first session of Q3 and the second half of 2026 — as new Fed Chair Kevin Warsh's global debut overshadowed a soft-but-still-expansionary macro data slate. Speaking at the ECB's Sintra forum in Portugal (his first international appearance roughly six weeks after taking the Fed's helm), Warsh said "we've seen that prices are too high," a hawkish signal that tempered rate-cut hopes. That came alongside a cooler ADP private payrolls print (+98,000 in June vs. +110,000 expected, down from +122,000 in May) and an ISM Manufacturing PMI that eased to 53.3 from 54.0 in May, still marking a sixth straight month of expansion. The S&P 500 (SPY) slipped -0.14%, the Nasdaq 100 (QQQ) led losses at -1.52% as chip and software names gave back part of their best-quarter-since-2020 rally, the Dow (DIA) closed essentially flat at +0.00%, and small caps (IWM) fell -0.38%. Treasuries sold off (TLT -1.04%, implying higher long-end yields) while gold firmed (GLD +0.60%) on the more hawkish tone. Beneath the index-level softness, breadth stayed constructive: 886 stocks remained in confirmed uptrends and 61 names registered volume-surge breakouts (avg +5.8% move on 2.4x relative volume), led by Real Asset Acquisition Corp (RAAQ +24.26%, the SPAC pursuing a merger with IQM Quantum Computers), Freedom Holding (FRHC +20.42%, Turkish bank acquisition approval), and Progress Software (PRGS +16.62%, a Q2 earnings beat carried over from Tuesday night). Sector leadership rotated defensively into Communication Services (+2.61%) and Financials (+1.61%), while Technology (-2.35%) and Industrials (-2.28%) lagged. Regular-session earnings reactions were a major theme: General Mills (GIS +8.53%), FactSet (FDS +6.72%), and MSC Industrial (MSM +3.69%) all beat estimates before today's open. After the close, small-cap reporters Greenbrier (GBX), Culp (CULP), Bassett Furniture (BSET), and Franklin Covey (FC) posted mixed results — none large enough to clear Finviz's $10B+ after-hours screener, but Franklin Covey was down roughly -18% in thin, very early after-hours trading following a steep EPS miss, with its 5:00 PM ET earnings call still to come.
