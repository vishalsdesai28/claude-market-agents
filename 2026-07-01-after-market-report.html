📋 Executive Summary

U.S. equities closed mostly lower on Wednesday, July 1, the first trading session of Q3 and the second half of 2026, as profit-taking hit the mega-cap tech complex following a record-setting close in June. The S&P 500 (SPY) slipped -0.14%, the Nasdaq 100 (QQQ) led losses at -1.52% amid weakness in large-cap semiconductors and software names, the Dow (DIA) finished essentially flat at +0.00%, and small caps (IWM) declined -0.38%. Treasuries firmed with the 20+ Year Treasury ETF (TLT) down -1.04% in price terms, signaling higher long-end yields, while gold (GLD) advanced +0.60% as a partial safe-haven bid emerged. Beneath the index-level softness, breadth was healthy: 886 stocks remained in confirmed uptrends and 62 names registered volume-surge breakouts, led by Freedom Holding (FRHC, regulatory approval), Guardant Health (GH, insurance-coverage win), and Progress Software (PRGS, Q2 earnings beat). Sector leadership rotated toward Communication Services (+2.60%) and Financials (+1.63%), while Technology (-2.35%) and Industrials (-2.28%) were the session's laggards. Earnings were a key regular-session driver: General Mills (GIS, +8.53%), FactSet (FDS, +6.72%), and MSC Industrial (MSM, +3.69%) all beat estimates before the open, while Progress Software's (PRGS) Tuesday-night beat carried into a +16.62% regular-session gain today. No large-cap company is scheduled to report after tonight's close, consistent with the quiet, holiday-adjacent pre-Independence Day calendar.
